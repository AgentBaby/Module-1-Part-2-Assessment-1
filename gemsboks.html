GEMSBOK

The gemsbok, gemsbuck or South African oryx (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope in the genus Oryx. It is native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. Some authorities formerly included the East African oryx as a subspecies.

Gemsbok are depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia, where the current population of the species is estimated to be 373,000 individuals. It is known for its spectacular long, straight horns and distinct black and white markings on its face and legs.

These desert-dwelling antelopes can survive without drinking water for weeks, obtaining moisture from the plants, roots, and tubers they consume.
