COMMON WARTHOG

The common warthog (Phacochoerus africanus) is a wild member of the pig family Suidae found in grassland, savanna, and woodland in Sub-Saharan Africa. It is identifiable by the two pairs of tusks protruding from the mouth and the distinct warts on its face used for defense.

Warthogs are omnivorous, eating grasses, roots, berries, bark, fungi, insects, and occasionally carrion. They use their snouts and tusks to dig up nutritious underground roots and bulbs.

Although capable of running at impressive speeds to escape predators like lions and cheetahs, warthogs will slide backwards into abandoned aardvark burrows to defend themselves with their sharp tusks facing outward.
