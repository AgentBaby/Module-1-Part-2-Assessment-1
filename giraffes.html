GIRAFFE

The giraffe (Giraffa) is an African mammal, the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. It is traditionally considered to be one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies. They are easily distinguished by their exceptionally long neck and legs, and their distinctive coat patterns.

Giraffes inhabit savannahs and woodlands. Their food source is leaves, fruits, and flowers of woody plants, primarily acacia species, which they browse at heights most other herbivores cannot reach.

They are preyed upon by lions, leopards, spotted hyenas, and African wild dogs. Giraffes live in herds of related females and their offspring, or bachelor herds of unrelated adult males.
